Leonard Nimoy

Leonard Simon Nimoy (March 26, 1931 â€“ February 27, 2015) was an American actor, film director, photographer, author, singer, and songwriter. He was known for playing Spock in the Star Trek franchise, a character he portrayed in television and film for almost fifty years, from a pilot episode shot in late 1964 to his final film performance in 2013.

A few keypoints of his life:

1954 - he married actress Sandra Zober; they had two children, Julie (born in 1955) and Adam (born Aug 9, 1956).

1964 - he made his first appearance in the rejected Star Trek television pilot "The Cage", and went on to play the character of Spock until the end of the production run in early 1969, followed by eight feature films and guest appearances in the various spin-off series.

1970s - he studied photography at the University of California, Los Angeles.

1973 - Nimoy made his directorial debut with the "Death on a Barge" segment for an episode of Night Gallery during its final season.

1975 - authored the autobiography called I Am Not Spock, which was controversial, as many fans incorrectly assumed Nimoy was distancing himself from the Spock character.

1977 - Nimoy earned an MA in education from Antioch College.

1984 - directed Star Trek III: The Search for Spock, and Star Trek IV: The Voyage Home in 1986.

1989 - he married his second wife, actress Susan Bay, cousin of director Michael Bay,

1995 - was involved in the production of Primortals, a comic book series published by Tekno Comix about first contact with aliens, which had arisen from a discussion he had with Isaac Asimov. There was a novelization by Steve Perry.

2000 - he received an honorary doctorate from Antioch University in Ohio, awarded for activism in Holocaust remembrance, the arts, and the environment.

2015 - dies at the age of 83.
